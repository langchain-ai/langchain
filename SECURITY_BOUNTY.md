
# LangChain Bug Bounty Program

## Introduction
LangChain welcomes security researchers to help us identify and fix security vulnerabilities in our codebase. We are committed to maintaining the security and integrity of our software and appreciate your efforts to help us improve.

## Reporting Security Issues

### How to Report
If you discover a security vulnerability in LangChain, please report it responsibly by:

1. **Emailing** security@langchain.com
2. **Using our secure vulnerability reporting form** (if available)

### What to Include
When reporting a vulnerability, please include:

- Description of the vulnerability
- Steps to reproduce the issue
- Proof of concept (if possible)
- Any relevant code snippets or configuration details
- Your contact information

### Expected Response Time
We will acknowledge your report within **24 hours** and provide a detailed investigation timeline.

## Bug Bounty Rewards

### Reward Tiers
We offer rewards for reported vulnerabilities based on their severity:

- **Critical**: $5,000 - $15,000 USD
- **High**: $1,000 - $5,000 USD
- **Medium**: $200 - $1,000 USD
- **Low**: $50 - $200 USD

### Eligibility
- Vulnerabilities must be previously unknown to us
- You must provide a clear, reproducible proof of concept
- The vulnerability must be valid and not already reported
- You must agree to a reasonable disclosure timeline

## Disclosure Policy

### Timeline
- We will investigate reported vulnerabilities within **7 days**
- We will coordinate with you during the investigation
- We will disclose fixes within **30 days** of validation

### Public Disclosure
- We will publicly disclose vulnerabilities after a reasonable period
- We will credit researchers in our changelog and security advisories
- We will not disclose details of vulnerabilities until they are fixed

## Scope

### In Scope
- Code vulnerabilities in LangChain core libraries
- Security issues in our documentation
- Vulnerabilities in our public APIs
- Issues affecting our hosted services

### Out of Scope
- Issues in third-party dependencies
- Configuration or usage errors
- Denial of Service (DoS) attacks that don't affect security
- Issues already reported and fixed

## Legal Notice
By participating in this bug bounty program, you agree to:
- Not engage in any activity that could harm LangChain or its users
- Not test any vulnerabilities that could cause data loss or unauthorized access
- Follow all applicable laws and regulations
- Provide accurate and complete information about vulnerabilities
